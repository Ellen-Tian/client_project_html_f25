Resume Support

Craft a Resume that Tells Your Story

Your resume is often your first impression—an opportunity to highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences to potential employers. Whether you’re starting from scratch or polishing an existing draft, these resources will help you every step of the way.

Need feedback? You can always meet with a CDO Career Coach to review your materials.

Resume Writing Made Easy

  1. Download a UMSI Template: Start with a format that works.
  2. Gather Your Experiences: Include jobs, internships, coursework, and projects.
  3. Write Impactful Bullets: Use action verbs and show results.
  4. Tailor for Each Opportunity: Adjust to fit the job description.
  5. Review and Edit: Proofread carefully and ask for feedback.

Resume Resources

  • UMSI Resume Guide and Rubric – Tips, formatting rules, and sample accomplishments.
  • Writing Effective Resume Bullets – Learn how to describe your work in ways employers notice.
  • Resume Rubric – Evaluate your resume using the same criteria as the CDO.
  • Application Materials Workshops – Attend a Resume Refresh workshop or review video tutorials.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be? For most students, one page is best. Experienced professionals may need two pages.
  • Do I need a different resume for each application? Yes—tailor your resume to match the opportunity.
  • Is it okay to include coursework or projects? Absolutely, especially if they demonstrate relevant skills.
  • How often should I update my resume? Every semester or after major projects and new experiences.
